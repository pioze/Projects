Homemade Biscuits

Learn how to make fluffy and delicious homemade biscuits with this easy recipe.

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 12 minutes
  • Total Time: 27 minutes
  • Yields: 12 biscuits

Protein Levels: Each serving of Homemade Biscuits contains approximately 3 grams of protein.

Advantages of Biscuits:

  • Provides energy and carbohydrates for sustained activities.
  • Offers essential minerals such as iron and calcium.
  • Can be a versatile base for various sweet or savory toppings.
  • Great for breakfast or as a side dish for meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually pour in the milk while stirring the mixture with a fork. Mix until the dough comes together and pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
  5. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface. Gently knead the dough a few times to ensure it is well combined.
  6. Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/2 inch. Use a biscuit cutter to cut out biscuits from the dough. Place the biscuits onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes or until they are golden brown on top.
  8. Remove the biscuits from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Enjoy!

Serve these homemade biscuits warm with butter, jam, or your favorite toppings. They make a delightful addition to breakfast or as a side for any meal.
