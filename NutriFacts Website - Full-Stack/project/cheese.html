Homemade Ricotta Cheese

Learn how to make delicious homemade ricotta cheese with this easy recipe.

  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: 25 minutes
  • Yields: 2 cups

Protein Levels: Each serving of Homemade Ricotta Cheese contains approximately 7 grams of protein.

Advantages of Ricotta Cheese:

  • Rich source of high-quality protein, which is essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Contains essential amino acids necessary for various bodily functions.
  • Good source of calcium, promoting strong bones and teeth.
  • Provides phosphorus, important for energy production and bone health.
  • Offers trace amounts of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin A and zinc.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the whole milk, heavy cream, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it reaches a temperature of 190°F (88°C), stirring occasionally to prevent scorching.
  2. Once the mixture reaches the desired temperature, remove the pot from heat and add the lemon juice. Stir gently for 1 minute to distribute the lemon juice evenly.
  3. Cover the pot with a clean kitchen towel and let it sit undisturbed for 1 hour. During this time, the curds will separate from the whey.
  4. Line a fine-mesh sieve or colander with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel. Place the sieve or colander over a large bowl to catch the whey.
  5. Gently pour the curds and whey into the lined sieve or colander, allowing the whey to drain off. Let the ricotta cheese drain for 1 hour or until it reaches your desired consistency.
  6. Transfer the ricotta cheese to a container and refrigerate until ready to use. Homemade ricotta cheese can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Enjoy!

Homemade ricotta cheese is perfect for spreading on toast, using in pasta dishes, or adding to your favorite recipes. Enjoy the creamy and delicious flavor of freshly made ricotta!
